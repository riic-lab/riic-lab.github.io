Research at RIIC

One of the most exciting avenues opened by advances in deep learning is the ability to compress complex, high-dimensional data into digestible concepts that humans can meaningfully interact with. In intensive care and anesthesia, this is especially valuable: clinicians must make rapid, high-stakes decisions while drawing from a flood of information, including vital signs, laboratory trends, imaging, medications, and procedural context.

At RIIC, we seek to construct and evaluate representational embeddings that distill this diversity into clinically relevant structures. By paying close attention to the latent space of deep learning models, we can further detect shifts in data distributions and, by identifying and targeting these, design applications that adapt to changing clinical environments and improve continuously over time.

Lab members

Dr. Camila González

Camila González is the Principal Investigator of the Representational Intelligence for Intensive Care (RIIC) Lab, where she leads research on machine learning applications for intensive care and perioperative medicine. She completed her PhD at the Technical University of Darmstadt and worked as a postdoctoral scholar at Stanford Medicine, where she developed dynamic learning and monitoring methods for clinical settings with ongoing data collection. Her research has received multiple distinctions, including the MICCAI Young Scientist Award, the François Erbsmann Award at the IPMI conference, the BVM Award from the German Conference on Medical Image Computing, and the Freunde der TU Darmstadt Award for Outstanding Scientific Achievements. Her work has also been featured in outlets such as Computer Vision News and the AI-Ready Healthcare podcast. Beyond her research, Camila has served as president of the MICCAI Student Board for two years and will take on the roles of Career Development and Student Chair for MICCAI 2026 and 2027. She is also a board member of the ContinualAI research society and webinar chair for IEEE EMBC.
